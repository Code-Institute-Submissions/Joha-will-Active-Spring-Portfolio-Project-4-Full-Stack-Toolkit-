How to use Sauna

  • Take a quick, post-workout shower prior to using the sauna.
  • To prevent dehydration, make sure to drink enough of water both before and after utilising any of these rooms. You could even carry a bottle of water with you so you can sip from it anytime you get thirsty.
  • Bring clean towels and sandals to the sauna.
  • Enter and exit quickly. Saunas are airtight, to keep the heat inside. Opening the door releases heat, and should be done expeditiously.
  • Don’t stretch out if the sauna is crowded.
  • If the temperature is too hot or cold for you, ask for a group consensus prior to adjusting the thermostat. Keep in mind that you can also adjust the temperature to your personal liking by changing your seat level.
  • Keep conversation low, and do not employ rowdy behavior. Saunas are designed for relaxation.
  • Lastly, you should always abide by the safety and hygiene rules to keep the rooms clean and safe for everyone else to enjoy. If you have a medical condition, do consult your doctor before entering these rooms.
